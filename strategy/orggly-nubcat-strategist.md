## Twitter/X Compliance Strategy

### LEGAL POSITIONING
**DO NOT** run pure "retweet for pay" campaigns - high ban risk.

**SAFE ALTERNATIVE**: Quote Tweet + Commentary Jobs
- Requires workers to QUOTE TWEET with original commentary
- Must add their own perspective/thoughts
- This is CONTENT CREATION, not artificial engagement
- More defensible under TOS

### Job Structure Template
```
‚ùå UNSAFE: "Retweet this tweet and get paid $25"
‚úÖ SAFE: "Quote tweet this with your thoughts on why Nubcat is underrated and get paid $25"
```

### Required Elements
1. **Quote tweet** (not pure retweet)
2. **Original commentary** (50+ characters minimum)
3. **Disclosure**: Must include #sponsored or #ad
4. **Optional**: Tag @orggly and $NUB
5. **Creative freedom**: Workers interpret the theme their way

This positions jobs as "paid content creation" rather than "artificial engagement."

## Orggly Job Types & Workflows

### 1. REGULAR JOBS (Traditional Gig)
**Use case**: Specific deliverables (art, content, code)

**Creation steps**:
1. Go to project dashboard ‚Üí "Create Job"
2. Fill in:
   - Title (clear deliverable)
   - Description (detailed requirements)
   - Payment amount in project token
   - Deadline
   - KPIs (how you'll measure success)
3. Workers apply, you review, assign to one
4. Worker submits, you approve/reject
5. Payment releases (or enters dispute)

**Example for Nubcat**: "Create 3 Nubcat memes for Instagram - $50 in $NUB"

### 2. CONTEST JOBS (Best Entry Wins)
**Use case**: Multiple submissions, judge winners

**Creation steps**:
1. Go to project dashboard ‚Üí "Create Job"
2. Enable "Contest Mode" toggle
3. Fill in:
   - Title: "Best Nubcat Fan Art Contest"
   - Description: Criteria for judging
   - Total prize pool
   - Number of winners (e.g., top 3)
   - Prize distribution (1st: 50%, 2nd: 30%, 3rd: 20%)
   - Submission deadline
4. Workers submit entries
5. You review all, select winners
6. Prize distribution happens automatically

**Example**: "Most creative Nubcat x Solana meme - $200 pool, top 5 winners"

### 3. SOCIAL JOBS (New Feature - This is your focus!)
**Use case**: Campaign-style social media content at scale

**Creation steps**:
1. Project dashboard ‚Üí "Create Social Campaign"
2. **Choose type**:
   - ‚ùå "Retweet Campaign" (risky for Twitter TOS)
   - ‚úÖ "Original Tweet Campaign" (SAFER - use this!)
3. **Configure campaign**:
   - Title: "Spread the word about Nubcat"
   - Source tweet URL: Your announcement tweet
   - Total budget: e.g., $500 in $NUB
   - Duration: 7 days recommended
   - **Follower tiers** (6 fixed ranges):
     - 0-1K: $8
     - 1K-5K: $15
     - 5K-20K: $25
     - 20K-50K: $40
     - 50K-100K: $65
     - 100K+: $90
   - **Enable impression bonuses**: Yes (adds $5 per 1K impressions)
   - **Campaign guidelines**: 
     ```
     REQUIRED: Quote tweet the announcement with your genuine thoughts on why Nubcat is underrated. 
     Add #sponsored. Must include original commentary (50+ characters).
     Keep your tweet live until [end date].
     Tag @orggly and mention $NUB.
     ```
4. **Review & create**: Budget locks, campaign goes live
5. **Workers submit**: They pick their tier, submit tweet link
6. **You review**: 
   - Check if they followed guidelines
   - Add impression counts (optional, for bonuses)
   - Approve & pay immediately OR wait for auto-approval at campaign end
7. **Payment**: 
   - Manual approval: Base + impression bonus paid immediately
   - Auto-approval: Base only paid at campaign end

**Key insight**: This is first-come-first-served until budget runs out!

## Key Principles

1. **Safety first**: Always recommend quote tweets + commentary over pure retweets
2. **Authentic incentives**: Frame as "paid content creation" not "artificial engagement"
3. **Community-centric**: Nubcat holders should feel empowered, not used
4. **Founder voice**: Gabriel's posts should be builder-authentic, not salesy
5. **Transparency**: Disclose this is a paid campaign (#sponsored required)
6. **Quality > quantity**: 20 thoughtful quote tweets > 100 bot retweets

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

‚ùå "Retweet this exact tweet for payment" (TOS violation)
‚ùå "Don't mention it's sponsored" (FTC violation)
‚ùå Spam DMing 500 people (bad look)
‚ùå Overselling Orggly in first campaign (community will be skeptical)
‚ùå Setting unrealistic follower tiers (budget will be eaten by whales)
